From Zero to Launch: Building a 20-Page Website for Peak Custom Solutions

By Olivia Arkema · June 22, 2026

When Rob first reached out, his business was thriving offline but had very little web presence to show for it. Peak Custom Solutions sells and services agriculture equipment, machinery, and specialty powersports vehicles, and he needed a website capable of representing all of it: rotating inventory, multiple product lines, and a clear path for customers to get in touch. I built the site from the ground up to meet each of those needs.

The Scope of the Project

This was not a template-and-go job. I designed and developed 20 fully custom pages covering Rob's product categories, service offerings, company story, and contact information. Every page was built with his customers in mind, from the layout and copy to the navigation structure and calls to action.

The site needed to serve two audiences well: buyers browsing for equipment they could trust, and Rob himself, who needed something he could maintain without dedicating hours to it each week.

A Listings Page That Works Automatically

The dynamic listings page is one of my favorite features of this project. I built it with an automated script that refreshes Rob's available inventory on a weekly basis, entirely on its own. No manual logins, no data entry, no risk of customers calling about equipment that sold weeks ago.

For a business where inventory turns regularly and the details matter, that kind of automation is more than a convenience. It frees Rob to focus on his customers and his operation rather than his website, while ensuring shoppers always see an accurate picture of what is available.

Contact Forms Built for Real Users

I integrated contact forms throughout the site so visitors always have a clear next step, whether they are interested in a specific piece of equipment, have a service question, or want to request a quote. The forms are built with a clean layout and mobile-friendly design, so customers can reach out just as easily from the field as from a desk.

Letting the Customers Do the Talking

I also developed a dedicated section for customer testimonials. In industries like agriculture and powersports, trust is everything. Featuring real customer voices prominently on the site gives prospective buyers something to connect with before they ever pick up the phone, and it reinforces the reputation Rob has already built in his community.

The Result

Rob now has a polished, professional website that brings all three sides of his business together under one cohesive brand. His listings stay current without any manual effort, his customers have a seamless way to reach him, and his reputation is front and center.

It is a site built not just to look good, but to work hard — which feels fitting for the kind of business Rob runs.
